SEGMENT BEHAVIORAL NOTES
Reference only. Use this to inform copy tone and hook selection. Do not copy these lines directly into emails.

---

1. Product Managers (REAL behavior)

They:
- send voice notes to themselves sometimes
- leave things half-documented
- forget small ideas, not big ones

Messaging triggers:
- "Do you ever leave ideas half-written and never come back?"
- "Where do your random product thoughts actually go?"
- "Not everything is worth opening Notion for... right?"

Key insight:
They avoid effort. Typing feels like effort. The copy should never imply they need to change behavior or build a habit.

---

2. Creators (REAL behavior)

They:
- think of ideas, don't store them
- feel they had something but lost it
- don't want structured tools

Messaging triggers:
- "You probably had better ideas than what you posted"
- "Do you ever remember an idea... but not fully?"
- "Most ideas die before you even try to write them"

Key insight:
They don't want systems. They want low effort capture. The copy should never imply a workflow or process.

---

3. Researchers / thinkers (REAL behavior)

They:
- don't consistently take notes
- rely on memory more than they admit
- revisit things only when needed

Messaging triggers:
- "Do you actually go back to your notes?"
- "How much of your thinking never gets saved?"
- "You think more than you write -- right?"

Key insight:
They are inconsistent. Do not assume discipline. Do not write to the version of them who takes thorough notes.
